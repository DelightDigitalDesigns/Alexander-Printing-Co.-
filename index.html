Submitting Your Files

File Submission Guidelines

A little preparation goes a long way toward a perfect print job.

📐

Give Your Design Some Breathing Room

Make sure your design extends about ⅛ inch (0.125") beyond the edge on all sides. This extra space gets trimmed away during finishing and ensures nothing important gets cut off. Think of it as a safety margin around your artwork.

🔒

Keep Important Content Away From the Edges

Text, logos, and anything you don't want trimmed should stay at least ⅛ inch inside the final edge. Printing and cutting involve tiny variations — keeping your content centered protects it.

🖼️

Use High Resolution Images

Images should be at least 300 DPI at their final printed size. Images pulled from websites are usually only 72 DPI and will appear blurry when printed. If it looks sharp on your screen at full zoom it may still print poorly — when in doubt ask us.

🎨

Colors Print Differently Than Screens

Screens display color using light (RGB) while printers use ink (CMYK). Bright blues, greens, and oranges in particular can shift when printed. For the most accurate results set your file to CMYK color mode before sending.

📄

PDF is Our Preferred Format

Save your file as a print-ready PDF with fonts embedded. This preserves your layout exactly as designed regardless of what software we use to open it. We also accept JPG, PNG, and most common design file formats.

✅

Proof Before You Send

Take a few minutes to review your file carefully before submitting — check spelling, phone numbers, addresses, and that all images look sharp. Once a job is approved and goes to press changes aren't possible. A second set of eyes never hurts.

Not sure if your file is ready? Send it over anyway and we'll take a look. After 100 years we've helped a lot of people get print-ready.
